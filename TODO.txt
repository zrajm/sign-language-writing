** TODO Difference between writing and notation/transcription
   A system is considered a script or functional writing system if it is
   intended for general usage. Notations, on the other hand, are primarily
   developed for academic purposes ([Bentele 2002: 4--15][Bentele 2002],
   [Coulmas 1996], [DeFrancis 1989], [Miller 1994: 196ff][Miller 1994]).

** TODO Early descriptions of handshapes (from 800-hundreds)
   The first attempt to record handshapes on paper was made in the 7th century.
   These so-called "finger numbers" or "numerical gestures" were not yet an
   attempt to write sign languages.

   Later, in the 16th and 18th centuries, further attempts were made to teach
   deaf students spoken language using the "manual alphabet", "methodical
   signs", and "sign register".
